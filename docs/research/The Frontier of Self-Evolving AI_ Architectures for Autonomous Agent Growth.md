### The Frontier of Self-Evolving AI: Architectures for Autonomous Agent Growth

##### 1\. The Paradigmatic Shift: From Fixed Workflows to Self-Organizing Systems

In the current trajectory of artificial intelligence, a critical "insight ceiling" has emerged, defined by the restrictive trade-off between rigid, human-defined workflows and inefficient reactive loops. Traditional systems rely on manual reconfiguration to navigate environmental shifts, while flexible agentic loops frequently fail to distill reasoning into transferable structures, resulting in iterative execution redundancy. To penetrate this ceiling, we are transitioning toward proactive, self-evolving systems that treat the agentic architecture itself as a dynamic, learnable parameter. These systems move beyond episodic task execution to establish persistent cognitive growth.The following table delineates the technical delta between legacy automation and the next generation of autonomous intelligence:| Category | Traditional Software | Reactive LLM Agents | Self-Evolving Agents || \------ | \------ | \------ | \------ || **Persistent Memory** | Static/Session-based | Short-term context window | Long-term experiential memory || **Task Independence** | Human-triggered | Multi-step prompted | Autonomous goal-setting || **Outcome Adaptation** | Manual code updates | Iterative prompting | Co-evolution of semantics and topology || **Operational Continuity** | Synchronous/Reactive | Conversational | Proactive/Unsupervised |  
The target of these frameworks is the realization of Autonomous General Intelligence (AGI)—systems that optimize their own architectures through real-world interaction. This shift is technically anchored in the Hierarchical Variable Agent (HiVA) framework.

##### 2\. Hierarchical Variable Agents (HiVA): Co-evolving Semantics and Topology

Strategic autonomy in multi-agent systems (MAS) necessitates the simultaneous optimization of internal agent instructions (semantics) and the system’s organizational structure (topology). Optimizing semantic prompts in isolation fails to resolve efficiency bottlenecks stemming from suboptimal agent connectivity. HiVA resolves this by modeling the MAS as a self-organized graph that undergoes topological reconfiguration, evolving from a singleton into a specialized, complex collective.This evolution is governed by the  **Semantic-Topological Evolution (STEV)**  algorithm. Given that the discrete nature of natural language precludes traditional backpropagation, STEV employs  **textual gradients** —discrete-domain surrogates derived from  **diagnostic gradient generation from environmental feedback** . During each iteration, the system evaluates its performance and selects one of four topological evolution options:

* **Add:**  Introduce a specialized successor agent to decompose sub-tasks.  
* **Delete:**  Prune redundant or inefficient nodes.  
* **Connect to Aggregator:**  Establish a direct link to the decision-making node for high-priority routing.  
* **Do Nothing:**  Maintain the current configuration if performance has saturated.To optimize resource allocation, HiVA utilizes the  **Knowledge-Aware Bayesian-Bandit (KABB)**  mechanism for  **Multi-Armed Bandit-infused forward routing** . KABB dynamically constructs "execution subgraphs," activating only contextually relevant nodes rather than the entire system. Empirical results across dialogue, coding, Long-context Q\&A, and mathematical benchmarks demonstrate a 5–10% delta in task accuracy. This structural evolution serves as a form of long-term organizational memory, bridging the gap between static execution and lifelong experiential learning.

##### 3\. Experience-Driven Lifelong Learning (ELL) and Knowledge Internalization

Dynamic, unpredictable environments require agents to transcend episodic performance in favor of  **Experience-driven Lifelong Learning (ELL)** . While static models are limited by knowledge cutoffs, ELL agents treat every interaction as data for structural refinement, allowing them to adapt to institutional conventions and evolving software ecosystems without model retraining.The ELL framework is governed by four core principles:

* **Experience Exploration:**  Agents navigate interdependent tasks and generate rich trajectories via self-motivated environmental interaction.  
* **Long-term Memory:**  Persistent structuring of historical knowledge, domain expertise, and commonsense reasoning.  
* **Skill Learning:**  Pattern abstraction from experience into reusable, refined strategies.  
* **Knowledge Internalization:**  The transformation of explicit experiences into implicit, intuitive "second nature" capabilities.These capabilities are rigorously evaluated via the  **StuLife**  benchmark, which simulates a longitudinal college journey to drive shifts from "Imitation to Learning" and "Passive to Proactive" behaviors. A critical strategic finding is the efficacy of  **Context Engineering over Model Scaling** . The results indicate that the design of an agent’s cognitive framework—structured via system prompts and memory architectures—is as vital to AGI as raw parameter count. This principle is particularly evident in the domain of autonomous computer use.

##### 4\. Specialized Frameworks for Self-Evolution: SEAgent and LASER

Autonomous interaction with novel software and high-resolution Graphic User Interfaces (GUIs) represents a major frontier where human annotations are often absent. Agents in these environments frequently fail to ground linguistic reasoning in visual reality.The  **SEAgent**  framework addresses this via a  **World State Model**  for step-wise trajectory assessment and a  **Curriculum Generator**  that manages task complexity through iterative trial-and-error. On the OS-World benchmark, SEAgent achieved a significant success rate improvement from 11.3% to 34.5%.Simultaneously, the  **LASER**  framework optimizes GUI grounding by endowing models with multi-step perception capabilities. By integrating  **Monte Carlo quality estimation**  with  **Intersection-over-Union (IoU)**  evaluations, LASER focuses on instruction-relevant key regions while adaptively allocating reasoning steps. LASER achieved a  **55.7 score on the ScreenSpot-Pro benchmark** , establishing a new state-of-the-art (SoTA) for 7B-scale models. These specialized visual evolutions directly enhance the reasoning depth of general-purpose research agents.

##### 5\. Writing as Reasoning: The WARP Framework and AgentCPM-Report

Traditional research agents often adhere to a "plan-then-write" paradigm, which is limited by the quality of a static initial outline. However, human-level research is a "knowledge-transforming" process where insights emerge through the articulation of arguments. The  **Writing As Reasoning Policy (WARP)**  treats writing as a reasoning mechanism to reveal logical gaps.WARP alternates between two macro-states:

* **Evidence-Based Drafting:**  Converting the structural plan into substantiated content.  
* **Reasoning-Driven Deepening:**  Utilizing the draft as context to detect superficial arguments and trigger targeted outline expansion.To equip 8B-parameter models with these capabilities, a  **Multi-Stage Agentic Training**  strategy is required: SFT for cold-start format adherence, Atomic Skill RL for local actions, and Holistic Pipeline RL for global quality. A critical component is the  **Trajectory Pruning**  mechanism, which solves the "optimal stopping problem." Research shows that teacher models generate strong drafts but make suboptimal termination decisions; pruning teacher trajectories at the observed  **9-step performance plateau**  provides the student with a cleaner termination signal.This specialized inference paradigm allows the  **AgentCPM-Report (8B)**  to match or outperform massive proprietary systems, including  **Gemini-2.5-Pro-deepresearch**  and  **OpenAI-deepresearch** , particularly on the "Insight" metric. This confirms that for 8B models, SFT is a necessary cold start because small-scale models initially struggle to determine informational saturation without anchored trajectories.

##### 6\. Skill Self-Evolution: AutoSkill, SkillRL, and ALAS

To achieve scalable growth, agents must distill noisy interaction histories into a  **SKILL-BANK** —a library of high-level, reusable capabilities that can be injected into requests without retraining. Skill distillation achieves  **10–20× token compression**  compared to raw trajectories while increasing reasoning utility.The SKILL-BANK differentiates between two knowledge tiers:

* **General Skills:**  Universal strategies such as systematic search patterns, state verification, and prioritizing unvisited nodes.  
* **Task-Specific Skills:**  Specialized heuristics and category-particular sequences (e.g., specific protocols for cleaning or datainterpretive tasks).The  **SkillRL**  framework employs a  **Recursive Evolution**  mechanism where the skill library co-evolves with the policy during reinforcement learning. By analyzing failure modes after each epoch to generate new skills, SkillRL achieves faster convergence and a  **15.3% performance improvement**  over baselines. Similarly, the  **ALAS (Autonomous Learning Agent System)**  modular pipeline utilizes  **web retrieval and distillation**  to generate a  **learning curriculum**  for post-cutoff data. This approach raises question-answering accuracy from  **15% to 90%**  in rapidly evolving domains, transforming ephemeral interaction into machine wisdom.

##### 7\. Strategic Synthesis: The Future of Autonomous Discovery

The collective breakthroughs in HiVA, WARP, and SkillRL signal a fundamental shift in the AI roadmap: the path to AGI depends less on raw model scale and more on the design of effective cognitive, topological, and skill-based planning processes. These frameworks demonstrate that  **8B models**  represent the new frontier for high-performance,  **privacy-preserving, local-first**  research.By enabling agents to run on-device while maintaining parity with proprietary giants, organizations can conduct deep inquiry on private data without external security exposure. Specialized fields like biomedical discovery—supported by the  **STELLA**  framework—will be the primary beneficiaries of agents that independently adapt reasoning modules to complex experimental data. The strategic takeaway is clear: the industry is transitioning from agents that merely follow instructions to autonomous systems that evolve through experience, converting every interaction into a permanent foundation of machine intelligence.
